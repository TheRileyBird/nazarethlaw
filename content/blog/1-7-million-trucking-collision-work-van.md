## Case Overview

Attorney Neil Nazareth of the Nazareth Law Firm successfully resolved a serious trucking accident case in the Eastern District of Louisiana, securing a **$1.7 million settlement** for the injured plaintiff. Because the settlement is confidential, the names of the parties cannot be disclosed. This case illustrates the catastrophic harm that can result when a large commercial vehicle operates negligently in traffic.

## The Incident

The collision occurred when an 18-wheeler tractor-trailer made an illegal left turn at an intersection, crossing over two lanes of traffic and crashing directly into a work van. The force of the impact was so violent that the work van was pushed all the way onto the neutral ground, throwing the driver into the side window and dashboard.

The reckless nature of the turn contributed to the severity of the crash:

- **Illegal Maneuver:** The truck driver executed a left turn that violated traffic laws, crossing multiple lanes without right of way
- **High Impact Force:** The weight and momentum of an 18-wheeler generate enormous force upon impact, far exceeding what a work van can absorb
- **Loss of Vehicle Control:** The van was displaced entirely from the roadway, reflecting the extreme violence of the collision
- **Driver Ejection Within Cabin:** Being thrown against the interior of the vehicle at high velocity causes serious blunt-force trauma

## Injuries Sustained

The plaintiff sustained multiple serious injuries as a direct result of the collision, including dental trauma, severe cervical spine damage, and significant lumbar spine pathology requiring extensive surgical treatment.

### Dental Injuries

The plaintiff suffered several cracked teeth as a result of the impact. Dental trauma from motor vehicle accidents can involve:

- Fractured or cracked teeth requiring restoration or extraction
- Damage to surrounding bone and soft tissue
- The need for crowns, bridges, implants, or other restorative procedures
- Ongoing dental pain and sensitivity
- Costs associated with long-term dental care

### Cervical Spine Injuries

The plaintiff's neck was severely injured in the collision, ultimately requiring a **two-level spinal fusion surgery**. Cervical fusion is a major surgical procedure involving:

- Removal of damaged or herniated discs between vertebrae
- Permanent joining of two vertebral levels using bone graft material
- Stabilization with metal plates, screws, or rods
- A lengthy recovery period requiring physical therapy and rehabilitation
- Ongoing monitoring for adjacent segment disease and potential future surgery

Cervical spine injuries can result in:

- Chronic neck pain and stiffness
- Headaches and radiating pain into the arms
- Numbness and tingling in the hands and fingers
- Limited range of motion
- Permanent impairment of daily activities

### Lumbar Spine Injuries

The plaintiff also sustained significant damage to the lumbar spine, requiring multiple interventions:

**Epidural Steroid Injections (ESIs):**

- Used to reduce inflammation and manage nerve pain
- Multiple injections were required, reflecting the severity and persistence of symptoms
- ESIs provide temporary relief but do not address underlying structural damage

**Laminectomy Surgery:**

A laminectomy is a spinal decompression procedure in which a portion of the vertebral bone (the lamina) is removed to relieve pressure on the spinal cord or nerve roots. This procedure:

- Is typically recommended when conservative treatment has failed
- Requires hospitalization and general anesthesia
- Involves a significant recovery period
- Carries risks including infection, nerve damage, and the need for repeat procedures

Lumbar injuries and the need for both injections and surgery indicate a severe level of damage causing:

- Chronic lower back pain
- Radiating pain down the legs (sciatica)
- Difficulty standing, walking, and performing basic activities
- Reduced quality of life and potential for long-term disability
- Inability to return to physically demanding work

## Commercial Truck Accident Liability

Cases involving 18-wheelers require thorough investigation of multiple sources of liability:

### Driver Negligence

The driver's illegal left turn constitutes clear negligence, and may also involve:

- Failure to observe and yield to oncoming traffic
- Inattention or distraction at the time of the turn
- Inadequate training on safe operation in traffic
- Hours-of-service fatigue

### Trucking Company Liability

The employing trucking company may be held responsible for:

- Vicarious liability for the driver's negligent conduct
- Negligent hiring, training, or supervision
- Failure to enforce safety policies and compliance with traffic laws
- Poor oversight of driver conduct and performance records

### Federal Trucking Regulations

Commercial trucking is regulated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA). Violations of FMCSA regulations, including hours-of-service requirements, driver qualification standards, and vehicle inspection mandates, can establish additional grounds for liability.
