## Case Overview

Attorney Neil Nazareth successfully resolved a significant motor vehicle collision case in New Orleans involving an 18-wheeler commercial truck, securing a **$1.7 million settlement** for the injured plaintiff. This case exemplifies the catastrophic consequences that can result when large commercial vehicles collide with passenger cars in urban traffic situations.

## The Incident

The collision occurred in central New Orleans when the plaintiff was lawfully executing a left-hand turn. At that critical moment, a large 18-wheeler commercial truck struck the plaintiff's vehicle, causing devastating injuries that would forever change the plaintiff's life.

Left-turn accidents involving commercial trucks are particularly dangerous because:

- **Size Differential:** The massive weight and size advantage of an 18-wheeler over a passenger vehicle
- **Impact Forces:** The tremendous force generated when a multi-ton truck collides with a smaller vehicle
- **Visibility Issues:** Commercial trucks have significant blind spots that can obscure smaller vehicles
- **Speed and Momentum:** Large trucks traveling at speed require much greater stopping distances
- **Urban Traffic Complexity:** Intersection accidents in busy urban areas often involve multiple factors including traffic signals, pedestrians, and congestion

## Catastrophic Injuries Sustained

The violent impact of the collision resulted in multiple serious injuries requiring extensive medical intervention:

### Cervical Spine Injuries

Injuries to the cervical spine (neck region) are among the most serious consequences of motor vehicle accidents. The plaintiff's cervical spine damage was severe enough to require surgical intervention with a 2-level cervical fusion.

**Cervical Fusion Surgery:**

A cervical fusion is a major surgical procedure where:

- Two or more vertebrae in the neck are permanently joined together
- Damaged or herniated discs are removed
- Bone grafts or artificial materials are used to fuse the vertebrae
- Metal plates, screws, or rods may be used to stabilize the fusion
- The procedure aims to eliminate pain from damaged discs and stabilize the spine

**Impact of Cervical Injuries:**

Cervical spine injuries can result in:

- Chronic neck pain
- Headaches and migraines
- Limited range of motion in the neck
- Numbness or tingling in arms and hands
- Weakness in upper extremities
- Potential for permanent disability
- Ongoing pain management needs
- Risk of adjacent segment degeneration requiring future surgery

### Lumbar Spine Injuries

The plaintiff also suffered damage to the lumbar spine (lower back region), which also required surgical treatment. Lumbar spine injuries are common in motor vehicle accidents due to the forces transmitted through the body during impact.

**Lumbar Surgical Procedures:**

Treatment for lumbar spine injuries may include:

- Discectomy to remove damaged disc material
- Spinal fusion to stabilize damaged vertebrae
- Laminectomy to relieve pressure on nerves
- Decompression procedures to address stenosis
- Installation of hardware to support the spine

**Consequences of Lumbar Injuries:**

Lower back injuries can cause:

- Chronic lower back pain
- Sciatica and leg pain
- Difficulty standing, walking, or sitting for extended periods
- Loss of mobility and flexibility
- Inability to lift or carry objects
- Impairment of daily activities
- Need for ongoing medical treatment
- Potential disability affecting employment

### Bilateral Knee Injuries

In addition to the devastating spinal injuries, the plaintiff sustained injuries to both knees. Bilateral knee injuries suggest the severity of the impact and the forces involved in the collision.

**Knee Injury Complications:**

Knee injuries from motor vehicle accidents can include:

- Ligament tears (ACL, MCL, PCL, LCL)
- Meniscus tears
- Fractures of the patella, femur, or tibia
- Cartilage damage
- Dislocations
- Soft tissue damage

**Long-Term Impact:**

Bilateral knee injuries can result in:

- Chronic pain in both knees
- Difficulty walking, climbing stairs, or standing
- Increased risk of arthritis
- Potential need for knee replacement surgery later in life
- Limitations on physical activities
- Impact on quality of life and independence

## Commercial Truck Accident Liability

Determining liability in commercial truck accidents involves examining multiple potential defendants:

### The Truck Driver

Driver negligence may include:

- Failure to yield the right of way
- Speeding or aggressive driving
- Distracted driving (cell phone use, eating, etc.)
- Fatigue from hours-of-service violations
- Inadequate training or experience
- Impairment from drugs or alcohol
- Failure to properly maintain awareness of surrounding traffic
- Violation of traffic laws

### The Trucking Company

Trucking companies may be liable for:

- Negligent hiring of unqualified drivers
- Inadequate driver training
- Failure to supervise drivers
- Encouraging or permitting hours-of-service violations
- Poor vehicle maintenance practices
- Inadequate safety policies and procedures
- Vicarious liability for driver's negligent actions

### Vehicle and Equipment Issues

Additional liability may arise from:

- Brake failures or defects
- Tire blowouts due to improper maintenance
- Defective mirrors or visibility equipment
- Lighting failures
- Steering or mechanical defects
- Improper vehicle inspections

### Third Parties

Other potentially liable parties may include:

- Vehicle manufacturers for defective parts
- Maintenance companies
- Cargo loading companies
- Government entities for dangerous road conditions

## Federal Trucking Regulations

Commercial truck operations are governed by extensive federal regulations including:

### Hours of Service Rules

- Limits on driving time before required rest breaks
- Maximum daily and weekly driving hours
- Mandatory rest periods
- Electronic logging device (ELD) requirements

### Vehicle Maintenance Standards

- Regular inspection requirements
- Brake performance standards
- Tire condition and tread depth requirements
- Lighting and visibility equipment standards
- Annual vehicle inspections

### Driver Qualifications

- Commercial Driver's License (CDL) requirements
- Medical certification standards
- Drug and alcohol testing programs
- Background checks and driving record reviews
- Training and experience requirements

Violations of these regulations can establish liability in accident cases.
