## Appointment to Distinguished Board

Neil Nazareth has been appointed to serve on the alumni association's Board of Directors at Loyola University New Orleans College of Law for the 2023-2024 term. This prestigious appointment recognizes Nazareth's distinguished career and his ongoing commitment to legal education and the law school community.

## Board Composition

The Loyola Law School Board of Directors includes several distinguished members of the judiciary and legal community:

- **Hon. Carl J. Barbier** - United States District Judge
- **Hon. Kern A. Reese** - Distinguished Jurist
- **Hon. June Berry Darensburg** - Respected Judge
- **Hon. Omar Mason** - Judicial Leader

Nazareth joins this esteemed group of legal professionals who guide the alumni association's initiatives and support the law school's mission of excellence in legal education.

## Connection to Loyola Law School

Neil Nazareth's relationship with Loyola University New Orleans College of Law spans many years and reflects his deep commitment to the institution:

### Legal Education

Nazareth earned his Juris Doctor degree from Loyola Law School, where he distinguished himself as a member of the prestigious Moot Court Board. Moot Court participation represents one of the highest honors for law students, requiring exceptional oral advocacy skills, legal writing ability, and dedication to the practice of appellate advocacy.

During his time as a law student, Nazareth also received the Law Excellence Award, recognizing his outstanding academic performance and contributions to the law school community.

### Ongoing Involvement

Beyond his role on the Board of Directors, Nazareth maintains active involvement with his alma mater through several channels:

**Fellow, Loyola Institute of Politics**

Nazareth serves as a Fellow in Loyola's Institute of Politics, an organization dedicated to promoting civic engagement, political discourse, and public service. Through this role, he contributes to discussions on important policy issues and helps prepare the next generation of civic leaders.

**Continuing Legal Education**

Nazareth has served as a CLE (Continuing Legal Education) instructor at Loyola, sharing his extensive experience in maritime law and personal injury litigation with fellow attorneys. His presentations have covered topics including maintenance and cure obligations and punitive damages in maritime cases.

**Mentor and Advisor**

Through various programs, including the Loyola University Business Executive Mentor Program, Nazareth provides guidance and mentorship to students and young professionals entering the legal field and business community.

## Significance of the Appointment

The Board of Directors plays a crucial role in supporting Loyola Law School's mission and connecting the alumni community. Board members:

- **Advance the Law School's Mission:** Support initiatives that enhance legal education and maintain Loyola's reputation for excellence
- **Engage Alumni:** Foster connections among graduates and create opportunities for networking and professional development
- **Support Students:** Contribute to scholarships, programs, and resources that benefit current law students
- **Promote the Legal Profession:** Uphold the values of the legal profession and encourage ethical practice
- **Guide Strategic Initiatives:** Provide input on important decisions affecting the law school and alumni association

## About Loyola University New Orleans College of Law

Loyola University New Orleans College of Law has a distinguished history of legal education dating back to 1914. The law school is known for:

- Strong emphasis on practical skills and experiential learning
- Commitment to public service and pro bono work
- Small class sizes and personalized attention
- Location in New Orleans, a unique legal jurisdiction with both civil and common law traditions
- Active clinical programs providing real legal services to underserved communities

The law school has produced numerous distinguished alumni who have made significant contributions to the legal profession, judiciary, business, and public service.
