## Case Overview

Attorneys Neil Nazareth and Jeremy Landry successfully resolved a premises liability matter within two years, securing a seven-figure settlement for a minor whose guardian fell from an apartment building that lacked proper safety railings on the balcony.

## The Incident

The case involved a serious fall from an apartment building balcony that did not have the required safety railings installed. This dangerous condition—the absence of protective barriers that are mandated by building codes—directly contributed to the tragic accident that resulted in severe injuries to the guardian of a minor child.

Balcony falls from multi-story buildings often result in catastrophic or fatal injuries due to the significant height involved. When property owners fail to install or maintain required safety features like railings, they create unreasonably dangerous conditions that put residents and visitors at serious risk.

## Premises Liability and Property Owner Duties

Under Louisiana premises liability law, property owners have a legal duty to maintain their properties in a reasonably safe condition and to protect visitors from unreasonable risks of harm. This duty includes:

- **Regular Inspections:** Property owners must regularly inspect their premises to identify hazardous conditions
- **Code Compliance:** Buildings must comply with applicable building codes and safety regulations, including requirements for balcony railings and other protective barriers
- **Prompt Repairs:** When dangerous conditions are discovered, property owners must take prompt action to correct them
- **Adequate Warnings:** If hazards cannot be immediately corrected, property owners must provide adequate warnings to prevent injuries

When a property owner fails to fulfill these duties and someone is injured as a result, the owner can be held liable for damages.

## The Importance of Balcony Safety Railings

Balcony railings serve a critical safety function in multi-story buildings. Building codes establish specific requirements for railing height, strength, and spacing to prevent falls. The absence of required railings on this apartment building balcony constituted a clear violation of building codes and created an unreasonably dangerous condition.

Safety railings are particularly important in residential buildings where:

- Children may be present and are especially vulnerable to fall hazards
- Residents and visitors may not be aware of the absence of protective barriers
- Falls from upper-story balconies can result in death or permanent disability
- The property owner has complete control over the installation and maintenance of safety features

## Building a Strong Premises Liability Case

The Nazareth Law Firm's success in this case demonstrates the importance of thorough investigation and documentation in premises liability matters. Building a compelling case typically requires:

- **Documenting the Hazard:** Photographs, videos, and expert inspections to establish the dangerous condition
- **Establishing Code Violations:** Demonstrating that the property failed to meet applicable building code requirements
- **Proving Causation:** Connecting the hazardous condition directly to the injuries sustained
- **Demonstrating Owner Knowledge:** Showing that the property owner knew or should have known about the dangerous condition
- **Calculating Damages:** Thoroughly documenting all injuries, medical expenses, lost income, pain and suffering, and other losses

The seven-figure settlement provides financial security for the minor plaintiff and ensures resources are available for any ongoing needs resulting from the incident.
